About Me

As a person born in the mid 1980s I have seen technology grow and evolve in ways that people in the past could only imagine. From watching my father read programming tips in News Rooms on the UseNet in the 80's, to learning to navigate the Internet with a dial-up modem in the 90's, to watching social media become the norm in the early 2000's, all the way up to now where smart tech lives in everyone's pockets, I've sat in wonder at these advancements. Although I've been witness to these changes over the years, I've never learned to participate, contribute, or help expand that technology. For years, I felt as if I didn't have the skills to learn how to become an active member of the tech community. That is, until I learned about Epicodus! After a some research, I decided that I do actually have not only the skills, but the smarts, the motivation, and commitment to learn how to become a programmer and developer. And I look forward to sharing this journey with you all, one that I hope will not end any time soon!

My Background

Though new to the world of programming and coding, I certainly have much experience elsewhere. Currently I earn a living by working at a local grocery store here in Portland. But that's not the career I am changing, it's simply a job to fit with my Epicodus schedule. The career I'm actually switching from is as a Mail Carrier (aka a Mail Man) for the United States Postal Service. While it certainly is a good job and one with it's numerous high points, I had to realize that not only was it not the right environment for me, but also not what I wanted to do for the rest of my life. No one would accuse me of being a genius, but I do feel that I have creative potential, and I wanted to pursue a career where I could use my intellect as a launching pad for my creativity. I carried mail for five years, and I don't regret a single day of it, but I am very pleased with my decision to make a change. And although I have some college experience, I never graduated. My hope is that Epicodus will help me get my employment eligibility caught up to what I've missed out on by not finishing my degree.


Before the post office, however, I worked a variety of different jobs. Going in reverse order, my work history has been:

  1. Meat Clerk at New Seasons Market
  2. Mail Carrier for the USPS
  3. Venue Security and Guest Services at the Aladdin Theatre
  4. Lead Bicycle Mechanic for Public Bikes in San Francisco, CA
  5. Supervisor and Record Buyer for Streetlight Records in Santa Cruz, CA

  • A webpage that imagines the Shire from Lord of the Rings as a vacation resort! The purpose of this page was to gain practice and experience with CSS stylings. Additionally, it was the first time throughout the course that my partner and I had written and styled a README
  • A webpage for a very rude cupcake shop. This was how my partner and I learned about using divs and spans!

Where I'm Heading

Beyond simply completing the full stack course at Epicodus, I have further goals in mind that I'd like to work towards.

  • A portfolio with personal projects that I've built outside of Epicodus.
  • Gain a certain level of proficiency with Python and C#, even though I am in the Ruby/React stack.
  • Make time for myself during this bootcamp. Remember to practice good habits.
  • Learn to recognize when I'm experiencing burn out or Impostor Syndrome, and learn to tools to cope with (what I'm told) are very common mental traps in the coding world.
